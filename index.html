Traffic Signals Display

Traffic Signal - Red

A red traffic signal indicates that vehicles must stop. It is one of the fundamental signals used to control traffic flow at intersections and ensure the safety of drivers, passengers, and pedestrians. The red light is a clear and universally recognized indicator to halt movement.

  • Stop Completely: When a traffic light turns red, all vehicles must come to a complete stop before the stop line, crosswalk, or intersection.
  • Safety Priority: The primary purpose of the red signal is to prevent accidents and collisions by clearly indicating when it is unsafe to proceed.
  • Legal Requirement: Failing to stop at a red light is a traffic violation that can result in fines, and increased insurance premiums.
  • Pedestrian Crossings: Red signals often coincide with pedestrian crossing signals, allowing pedestrians to cross the street safely.
  • Emergency Vehicles: In most regions, emergency vehicles are granted the right to proceed through a red signal after ensuring it is safe to do so. Regular drivers must yield to these vehicles.
Do's for Red Traffic Signals
  • Stop completely at the stop line, crosswalk, or intersection.
  • Prioritize safety by stopping smoothly and checking mirrors.
  • Observe pedestrian signals and give pedestrians the right of way.
  • Yield to emergency vehicles, even if the light is red.
  • Prepare for green by staying alert and ensuring the intersection is clear.
  • Check for right turn rules and proceed cautiously if allowed.
Don'ts for Red Traffic Signals
  • Don't run the red light, it's illegal and dangerous.
  • Don't block the intersection, ensure you can clear it completely.
  • Don't ignore no turn on red signs.
  • Don't rush through yellow, prepare to stop instead.
  • Don't distract yourself, stay focused at red signals.
  • Don't tailgate, maintain a safe distance from the vehicle ahead.

Traffic Signal - Yellow

A yellow traffic signal, also known as an amber light, serves as a warning that the traffic light is about to change to red. It is a crucial part of traffic control systems, indicating to drivers to slow down and prepare to stop if it is safe to do so. The yellow light provides a transition period between green and red signals to enhance road safety.

  • Prepare to Stop: The yellow light signals drivers to slow down and prepare to stop, as the light will soon turn red.
  • Clear the Intersection: Vehicles already in the intersection when the light turns yellow should continue through to clear intersection safely.
  • Do Not Speed Up: Drivers should not accelerate to try to "beat" the red light. This is dangerous and can lead to accidents.
  • Timing and Duration: The duration of the yellow light is typically calculated based on the speed limit and the width of the intersection.
  • Safety Buffer: The yellow signal acts as a buffer period, reducing sudden stops and preventing rear-end collisions.
Do's for Yellow Traffic Signals
  • When approaching a yellow light, reduce your speed and prepare to stop safely.
  • Proceed with caution if you are too close to the intersection to stop without causing a hazard.
  • Ensure you clear the intersection if you have already entered it when the light turns yellow.
  • Stay attentive to the changing signal and the actions of other drivers and pedestrians.
  • Prepare for green by staying alert and ensuring the intersection is clear.
  • Check for right turn rules and proceed cautiously if allowed.
Don'ts for Yellow Traffic Signals
  • Do not accelerate to try to beat the red light.
  • Ignoring the yellow signal and speeding through the intersection can lead to accidents and traffic violations.
  • Avoid stopping suddenly if you are too close to the intersection, as it can cause rear-end collisions.
  • Do not enter the intersection if the light has already turned yellow and you are not already committed to crossing.
  • Don't distract yourself, stay focused at yellow signals.
  • Don't tailgate, maintain a safe distance from the vehicle ahead.
Traffic Signal - Green

A green traffic signal indicates that vehicles are allowed to proceed through the intersection or continue their movement. It signifies that it is safe and lawful to move forward, ensuring the smooth flow of traffic.

  • Proceed With Caution : When the light turns green, drivers should proceed but remain vigilant for pedestrians, cyclists, and vehicles that may not have cleared the intersection yet.
  • Right Of Way :A green signal gives vehicles the right of way to move straight ahead, turn right, or make a left turn when it is safe and there is no oncoming traffic or pedestrian traffic.
  • Intersection Clearance :Ensure that the intersection is clear before proceeding, especially if the green light follows a long red light or heavy traffic flow.
  • Pedestrian Awareness :Watch for pedestrians who may still be crossing the street or just beginning to cross as the light changes.
  • Emergency Vehicles :Even on a green signal, always yield to emergency vehicles such as ambulances, fire trucks, and police vehicles with sirens on.
Do's for Green Traffic Signals
  • Move through the intersection smoothly and without hesitation if it is safe to do so.
  • Yield to oncoming traffic when making a left turn, unless you have a dedicated left-turn arrow.
  • Look out for pedestrians who might be finishing crossing the street.
  • Stay attentive to the changing signal and the actions of other drivers and pedestrians.
  • Maintain your lane and avoid sudden lane changes while crossing the intersection.
  • Check for right turn rules and proceed cautiously if allowed.
Don'ts for Green Traffic Signals
  • Do not assume the intersection is completely clear, always scan the area for late crossers or vehicles.
  • Avoid accelerating excessively when the light turns green; maintain a safe and controlled speed.
  • Do not enter the intersection if traffic is backed up on the other side .
  • Never ignore pedestrians at crosswalks; they always have the right of way when crossing legally.
  • Stay focused on driving and avoid distractions, even when the light is green.
  • Don't tailgate, maintain a safe distance from the vehicle ahead.
